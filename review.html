QUARKS – Design Thinking Project

SitRight Chair: A Posture Awareness System for Healthy Sitting Habits

Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering
Thiagarajar College of Engineering

Introduction

In today’s fast-paced digital world, people—especially students and professionals—spend long hours sitting. Poor sitting posture has quietly become a major health concern. The SitRight Chair, developed by Team QUARKS, aims to encourage healthy sitting habits through a simple, affordable, and user-friendly design. This project blends ergonomics and awareness to reduce problems like back pain, spinal stress, and fatigue that arise from incorrect sitting positions.

Objectives

  • Promote healthy posture habits and daily ergonomic awareness.
  • Create a low-cost posture-awareness system adaptable to any chair.
  • Ensure comfort, privacy, and simplicity in operation.
  • Align with UN SDG 3 – Good Health & Well-Being.
  • Demonstrate how electronics and design thinking improve lifestyle health.

Motivation

  • Problem: Prolonged sitting causes spinal imbalance and body fatigue.
  • Existing Solutions: Available chairs and wearable posture devices are costly and complex.
  • Need Gap: Lack of a budget-friendly, attachable, and privacy-safe posture-alert product.
  • Vision: Build an accessible and portable awareness system for schools, offices, and homes.
“The greatest threat to our planet is the belief that someone else will save it.” – Robert Swan

Problem Statement

Long study or work hours lead to slouching and incorrect posture, resulting in neck, back, and spine problems. There is a strong need for a cost-effective chair attachment that helps users recognize and correct posture in real time.

Voice of Customer

Purpose: To learn about user posture habits and expectations.
Method: Online surveys and direct discussions with students, faculty, and office staff.

  • 80% experience back or neck pain.
  • 70% lack posture awareness.
  • 65% prefer silent feedback (light / vibration).
  • 85% are willing to use an affordable posture-feedback system.

Conclusion: Users want a non-intrusive, comfortable, and low-cost solution that quietly improves posture awareness.

Brainstorming

Techniques Used: Mind-mapping • SCAMPER • Feedback sessions.

  • Camera-based posture detection.
  • Pressure-sensor seating pads.
  • Wearable posture trackers.
  • Attachable sensing system (selected direction).

Reason for Selection: Simple • Low cost • Non-contact • Portable • Privacy-safe.
